# Understanding Intend

Intend is both a task management app and a philosophy that fundamentally differs from traditional to-do list approaches. While most task management apps follow GTD (Getting Things Done) principles, Intend takes a different approach focused on intentionality and goal achievement.

## Core Philosophy

Intend differs from traditional task managers in four key ways:

### 1. Choosing & Doing over Organizing
- Traditional apps focus on managing and organizing tasks
- Intend focuses on actually doing things, not maintaining lists
- Avoids the trap of spending more time organizing tasks than completing them
- Discourages creating endless lists of things you may never work on

### 2. Aliveness instead of Exhaustiveness
- Traditional apps aim to capture everything so nothing is forgotten
- Intend recognizes that some things are worth forgetting or ignoring
- Prevents task lists from growing endlessly and becoming stale
- Tasks that aren't done today don't automatically carry forward
- Requires conscious decision to bring forward tasks, removing those that aren't truly important

### 3. Goals as Fundamental, rather than Tasks
- Traditional apps treat projects as buckets for tasks
- Intend starts with goals - you can't use the app without setting at least two
- Every task must be explicitly connected to a specific goal
- Makes it clear why you're doing what you're doing
- Helps avoid busy work that doesn't serve your larger purposes

### 4. Proactive, rather than Reactive
- Traditional apps ask "what should I do from all these possibilities?"
- Intend asks "what do I want to achieve and how can I get there?"
- Encourages creating momentum toward goals rather than reacting to incoming demands
- Supports creative and self-directed work where energy comes from within

## Key Features

### Goal-First Structure
- 3-10 main goals (each with a digit for quick reference)
- All tasks are tagged with their corresponding goal
- Regular prompts to evaluate if your actions match your intentions

### Daily Intentions
- Set specific intentions for each day
- Connect each intention to a specific goal
- End-of-day reflection on progress toward goals

### Momentum over Perfection
- Focus on regular progress rather than perfect planning
- Emphasis on doing something rather than nothing
- Regular assessment of whether actions align with goals

### Regular Reviews
- Built-in reflection process
- Evaluate if your daily activities are moving you toward your goals
- Opportunity to adjust strategy based on results

## User Benefits

- Clearer connection between daily tasks and long-term goals
- Less time spent organizing tasks, more time spent doing them
- Reduced guilt from endless stale tasks
- More intentional choices about where to focus time and energy
- Better alignment between stated priorities and actual behavior

Intend works particularly well for self-directed people like students, freelancers, and creative professionals who have significant control over how they spend their time and need to stay connected to their bigger picture goals.
